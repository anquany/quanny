The person behind the newsletter

Hi, I'm Anquan Clemmons.

I'm a sophomore in high school and an aspiring investment banker — and I started CapitalBrief because I couldn't find a finance resource that worked for everyone. Most of what's out there is either too basic to be useful or too dense to be readable.

This newsletter is my attempt to fix that. Whether you've never bought a stock in your life or you're actively tracking your portfolio, my goal is the same: make you sharper about how money actually moves.

I cover the mechanics of why stocks move, the big macro stories shaping the global economy — things like the possible AI bubble, conflict in the Middle East, Fed policy — and the foundational finance concepts that make all of it make sense. Every piece is written by me, researched by me, and held to the standard I'd want as a reader.

Outside of this, I'm working toward a spot at Michigan Ross and currently building my skills as a member of my school's investment club, where we research and pitch real investment ideas.

What you'll find here
Three types of content, one goal
📈
Stock analysis & market mechanics
What makes a stock move? Why did that company's earnings send shares up 15% — or down 20%? I break down the forces behind individual stocks and sectors in plain language.
🌍
Macro & current events
The AI bubble debate. Oil and geopolitics. Fed rate decisions. I cover the big economic stories that affect every investor — and explain why they actually matter for your money.
📚
Finance fundamentals
ETFs, bonds, options, valuation — the building blocks of financial literacy. Explained from first principles so you actually understand them, not just recognize the words.
Who reads CapitalBrief
Built for two kinds of readers

Complete beginners

People who already follow markets
Already have a brokerage account and track the news? CapitalBrief is a second opinion — a clear-eyed read on what's actually moving the needle versus what's just noise.
